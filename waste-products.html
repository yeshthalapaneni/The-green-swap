Topic 02

What are waste products?

Waste products are materials or items thrown away after use, including plastic packaging, electronic items, textile waste, and food leftovers.

When products are discarded without proper sorting or recycling, they often end up in landfills or pollute natural ecosystems. Some waste takes decades or centuries to break down.

Reducing waste products begins with small changes: choose reusables, avoid unnecessary packaging, repair before replacing, and separate recyclable materials correctly.

Types of waste products

Plastic waste

Bags, wrappers, and single-use containers with long decomposition times.

E-waste

Old electronics that may contain harmful materials if not handled properly.

Textile waste

Discarded clothes from fast fashion and low-durability product cycles.
