Topic 01

What is consumerism?

Consumerism is a social and economic pattern where people are encouraged to buy more goods and services, often beyond basic needs.

In many communities, consumerism is driven by advertising, social pressure, and rapid product cycles. When products are purchased too often and replaced too quickly, resources are consumed faster and waste increases.

Understanding consumerism helps us make better choices: buying durable items, repairing what we own, and prioritizing need over impulse.

Common effects of consumerism

Resource pressure

Higher demand increases extraction of water, energy, and raw materials.

Short product life

Frequent upgrades make many useful products become unwanted early.

Waste growth

More purchases usually mean more packaging and discarded goods.
